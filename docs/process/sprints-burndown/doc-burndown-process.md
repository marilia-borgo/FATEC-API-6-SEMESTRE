## Workflow

1. Planning  
With the stories already defined by the PO, the team participates in Sprint Planning to understand the sprint scope and break each story into smaller tasks. In this stage, the Scrum Master defines the initial estimate in hours for the planned tasks and distributes this plan across the working days/hours of the sprint (calculated by the Master).

2. During the sprint  
The developer’s role is only to log the actual hours worked on tasks that already have an estimate and to enter a DELIVERY EXPECTATION. Developers must not change the task time tracking control, meaning they must not modify the estimate already defined for the activity.

3. Task completion  
When finishing an activity, the task must follow the normal board flow until Done, so that sprint progress is correctly reflected in the reports. The burndown considers the board configuration, the estimate used, and the evolution of the items in the workflow during the sprint.

*Delivery Expectation is the date that the developer, upon reading the task, defines as when it should be completed.  
The date is used only for Scrum Master organization, with the purpose of avoiding bottlenecks.*

## Team standard

* Planned sprint task: receives an initial estimate in hours during Planning with no later additions or adjustments.  
* Developer: logs only the hours actually worked on Jira Tickets.  
* Developer: does not change the task estimate / time tracking control.  
* Hours must only be logged on Jira Tickets that already have a defined estimate.  
* Completed task: must be moved correctly to the final column of the workflow.  
* Unplanned technical tasks: are part of the scope, but do not receive an hour expectation for this control.  
* Hotfixes: are also part of the scope and do not receive an hour expectation.  
* Any doubts about hours, exceptions, or need for adjustments: contact the Scrum Master.  
* Tasks can be freely created during the Sprint (without adding time tracking control).
